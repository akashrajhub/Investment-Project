Overview

Project Overview

Residential property prices vary significantly based on location, house size, condition, and neighborhood characteristics. This project helps investors and decision-makers estimate fair property values, understand pricing risk, and identify high-potential investment opportunities using historical housing data.

The analysis focuses on reducing uncertainty in property valuation and highlighting cases where renovation or mispricing can create strong returns.

Dataset

21,000+ residential property sales records, individual transaction-level

Tools Used

Python · SQL · XGBoost · Ridge · Power BI · Tableau

Best Result

XGBoost R² 95.15% · RMSE 11.73% on held-out test data

Business Value

Renovation ROI signals · risk flagging · undervalued property identification

Modeling

Modeling & Statistical Validation

XGBoost was selected as the primary model for its ability to capture complex non-linear pricing patterns. Ridge Regression served as an interpretable benchmark for stakeholder communication.

Modeling Approach

1

Conducted detailed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand key price drivers and feature distributions.

2

Applied log transformations to reduce skewness and stabilize variance across price and size features.

3

Encoded zipcode categorically to capture neighbourhood-level pricing effects as a model signal.

4

Used OLS regression as a diagnostic tool to test feature significance and price driver relationships.

5

Built and compared 5 regression models: Ridge, Huber, ElasticNet, Random Forest, and XGBoost — evaluated on RMSE and R².

Insights

Key Insights

Findings from EDA, model outputs, and dashboard exploration that inform investment decision-making.

Most properties are priced close to expectations, indicating a broadly stable and predictable market.

Pricing uncertainty increases significantly for very small, very old, or unusual homes — higher investment risk.

Low-condition homes appear riskier due to low transaction volume and high price variation.

Standard, well-maintained homes show the most predictable pricing behavior — lower risk profile.

Properties that differ significantly from their neighborhood averages carry meaningfully higher investment risk.

Low-condition homes with strong location or large land area often present high-return renovation potential.

Recommendations

Business Recommendations

Actionable strategies for investors and decision-makers based on model outputs and dashboard analysis.

Investment Strategy Recommendations

Apply these to acquisition screening and portfolio decisions.

1

Use renovation potential signals from the dashboard to identify undervalued properties before market correction.

2

Focus renovation investments on low-condition homes with strong location fundamentals and large lot sizes.

3

Always compare target properties against neighbourhood averages before committing to a purchase price.

4

Apply caution when investing in very small or very old homes — model error is highest in these segments.

5

Prioritise standard property types for lower-risk investments with more predictable return profiles.

6

Deploy dashboards as a live decision-support tool during acquisition screening and portfolio review cycles.

Expected outcome: Improved pricing accuracy, better risk control, and higher ROI from renovation-driven investments.
